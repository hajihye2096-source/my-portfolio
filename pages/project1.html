Improving communication for multi-order deliveries

Role

Product designer

Team

Timeline

2024 Q3

Background

Coupang Eats introduced a new system to deliver 3 or more orders at once, aiming to increase revenue per order by processing more deliveries simultaneously. However, the existing delivery status screen could only display up to 2 orders, failing to properly accommodate the new delivery approach. This left customers struggling to get a clear overview when multiple deliveries were happening at the same time. We determined that improving the delivery status page to provide clearer real-time updates for multiple orders was essential to solve this problem.

Goal

User Goal

Users should clearly understand multiple delivery statuses and predict arrival order

Business Goal

Reduce delivery-related customer service inquiries and improve service reliability and satisfaction

Problem & Hypothesis

01

Multiple 'nearby store, nearby delivery' tags overlapped on one screen, confusing users about delivery status.

→Simplifying delivery status to focus on order will help users understand the current situation more easily.

02

Users couldn't tell which order would arrive first, making it hard to understand delivery stages.

→Visualizing each order's progress in stages with appropriate guidance will reduce customer cognitive load.

Solution Direction

We simplified the complex delivery information by organizing it according to order and context. This ensures users have a clear and consistent experience without confusion, even when dealing with multiple orders.

Order-based delivery structure

Reorganized the delivery process around sequence so users can understand multiple order progress at a glance.

Simplified status representation

Reduced unnecessary information and visual elements to make core stages clearly recognizable.

User-friendly messaging

Provided guidance through natural and personalized language to create familiar and trustworthy experiences even in multi-delivery situations.

User Test

Can users clearly understand delivery status and predict arrival order in a multi-delivery environment?

Figma interactive prototype testing, interviews and scenario-based task execution (explore–understand–predict)

Result

All 4 out of 4 participants understood both their own and other deliveries within the scenario

Reflection

We simplified information around order and context so users wouldn't get confused when multiple deliveries were happening simultaneously. This allowed us to provide clear and consistent delivery experiences even in multi-order situations.
