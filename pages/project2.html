Background

Coupang Eats had introduced various discount coupons, but both download rates (48%) and actual usage rates (7%) fell short of expectations. While customers were aware of the coupons' existence, they had difficulty clearly understanding whether "this coupon would actually apply to my order" or "under what conditions can I use it." This confusion was due to unclear information delivery about coupons and a mismatch between the actual usage experience and the guidance provided.

Insights

Customers recognized the existence of coupons but couldn't gain trust due to unclear specific conditions and application methods. This likely led to customers not completing their orders.

Insufficient coupon information

Despite customers knowing about coupons, key information like usage conditions, applicability, and expiration dates beyond just the discount amount wasn't sufficiently communicated.

Confusion in coupon application process

Downloaded coupons weren't automatically applied at the cart stage, leading customers to wonder "I definitely got the coupon, why can't I use it?" and lose trust.

Trust issues leading to abandonment

Due to this uncertainty, customers hesitate to complete orders or ultimately abandon at the payment stage, even when they have coupons.

Problem Definition

Customers know coupons exist but don't clearly understand usage conditions and applicability. This leads to decreased trust in coupons overall, resulting in lower usage and conversion rates.

Hypothesis

If customers can intuitively understand coupon conditions and application status, trust in coupon usage will be restored, leading to increased both download and actual usage rates.

Solution Direction

We made coupon information instantly understandable and immediately verifiable, ensuring customers feel confident they're in a 'guaranteed discount state.'
